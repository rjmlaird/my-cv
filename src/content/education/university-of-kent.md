- During my PhD work, I studied Jupiter-family comets as part of the international SEPPCoN collaboration, aiming to characterise the physical properties of cometary nuclei and compare them with other small body populations. I was responsible for processing and analysing optical imaging data from major observatories, including the VLT, Palomar, and Keck telescopes, and contributed to the interpretation of thermal-IR data from NASA’s Spitzer Space Telescope. My work provided critical insights into comet size, shape, spin, albedo, and composition, supporting spacecraft missions such as ESA’s Rosetta and NASA’s Stardust-NExT and EPOXI. Through this project, I gained extensive expertise in astronomical image processing, scientific analysis, and collaborative research within a large international team of scientists.
